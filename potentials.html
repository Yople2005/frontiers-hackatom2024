Where SMRs Should Be Constructed in Myanmar (Kyaukse)

Optimal Locations for SMR Deployment in Kyaukse

Kyaukse, as a key city in Myanmar, presents several promising locations for the construction of Small Modular Reactors (SMRs). The following factors should be considered when selecting sites for SMR deployment:

  • Proximity to Urban Areas: Sites near Kyaukse’s urban centers would ensure a reliable power supply for growing residential and commercial demands.
  • Access to Infrastructure: Locations with existing infrastructure, such as transportation and communication networks, would facilitate the construction and maintenance of SMRs.
  • Environmental Considerations: Sites with minimal environmental impact and good cooling water availability are ideal to minimize ecological disruption and enhance operational efficiency.
  • Community Acceptance: Engaging with local communities to ensure acceptance and support is crucial for the successful implementation and operation of SMRs.
How Much SMRs Can Supply

Power Supply Capabilities of Small Modular Reactors

Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) offer a flexible and scalable solution for energy production. Here’s how much power they can supply and their potential impact:

  • Power Capacity: Each SMR unit typically provides up to 300 MWe (megawatts electrical). Depending on the energy needs, multiple SMRs can be deployed at a single site, providing a cumulative capacity that can significantly contribute to the power grid.
  • Flexible Deployment: Due to their modular design, SMRs can be deployed incrementally to match growing energy demands, allowing for a more tailored and responsive energy supply.
  • Energy Security: SMRs can enhance energy security by providing a reliable and stable power supply, reducing dependence on fossil fuels and mitigating the risks of power shortages.
Positive Effects on Kyaukse

Benefits of SMRs for Kyaukse

The implementation of Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) in Kyaukse could bring numerous positive effects, contributing to both the local community and the broader region:

  • Economic Growth: The construction and operation of SMRs can create jobs and stimulate economic activity in Kyaukse, boosting local industries and services.
  • Enhanced Energy Access: SMRs can provide a stable and reliable power supply, reducing energy shortages and supporting the growth of new businesses and infrastructure in Kyaukse.
  • Environmental Benefits: By replacing or reducing the use of fossil fuels, SMRs can help decrease greenhouse gas emissions and improve air quality in the region.
  • Energy Independence: SMRs contribute to energy security and independence, reducing reliance on imported energy sources and enhancing regional self-sufficiency.
Energy Optimization

Utilizing Excess Nighttime Energy

Optimizing Energy Use

During periods of low energy demand at night, SMRs may generate more electricity than is immediately needed. To ensure that this surplus energy is used effectively and not wasted, it can be directed towards innovative solutions. One promising approach is to convert excess energy into mechanical energy for water pumping.

Energy Storage and Hydro Power Enhancement

By using surplus electricity to pump water to elevated reservoirs, we can store energy in the form of potential energy. This stored water can then be released to flow through hydro power plants during peak demand periods or in the summer, when energy needs are higher. This method not only helps in balancing energy supply throughout the year but also enhances the efficiency and reliability of hydro power plants.

Benefits for Kyaukse

The strategic location near the Zawgyi River provides an excellent opportunity for integrating hydro power solutions with SMR-generated energy. For Mandalay, where the population ranges from 100,000 to 120,000 and there are between 20,000 and 25,000 households, effective energy management is crucial. By leveraging excess nighttime energy to support hydro power, we can address seasonal energy demands more efficiently.

In particular, Kyaukse, a significant area within Mandalay, would need an estimated 10 MW of power to meet its electricity demands. This approach ensures that surplus energy from SMRs contributes to a stable and reliable power supply, enhancing energy resilience and sustainability for the region.

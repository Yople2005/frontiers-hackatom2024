Frequently Asked Questions

Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) are a type of nuclear reactor designed to be smaller in size and capacity compared to traditional nuclear reactors. They offer enhanced safety features, cost efficiency, and are suitable for various applications and locations.

SMRs can provide reliable and sustainable energy to underserved areas, reduce environmental impact, and offer cost-effective energy solutions. They can be particularly beneficial for rural and remote locations in Myanmar.

Yes, SMRs are designed with advanced safety features to minimize environmental risks. They produce lower levels of waste and emissions compared to traditional energy sources, making them a more eco-friendly option.

Key advantages of SMRs include enhanced safety, lower construction and operational costs, environmental benefits, and operational flexibility. They can be deployed in a variety of locations and adapted to different energy needs.

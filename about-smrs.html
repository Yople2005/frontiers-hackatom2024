About SMRs

What Are Small Modular Reactors?

Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) are advanced nuclear reactors that have a power capacity of up to 300 MWe per unit, which is about one-third of the generating capacity of traditional nuclear power reactors. SMRs are designed to be modular and scalable, which allows for the construction of multiple units at a site to meet energy demand as it grows.

SMRs offer several advantages over traditional reactors, including enhanced safety features, reduced costs, and the ability to be deployed in a wider range of locations, including remote and off-grid areas.

Advantages & Disadvantages

Pros and Cons of SMRs

Pros

  • Enhanced Safety: SMRs incorporate advanced safety features that minimize the risk of accidents.
  • Cost-Effective: The modular design and smaller size lead to reduced construction and operating costs.
  • Flexible Deployment: SMRs can be installed in locations unsuitable for larger reactors, including remote areas.
  • Scalability: Multiple SMRs can be added to a site as energy demand increases.

Cons

  • Regulatory Challenges: SMRs may face complex regulatory hurdles before deployment.
  • High Initial Costs: Despite overall cost savings, the initial development costs can be high.
  • Waste Management: Nuclear waste remains a concern, even with smaller reactors.
  • Public Perception: Nuclear energy, in general, faces public skepticism and opposition.
